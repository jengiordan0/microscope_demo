What is a Light Microscope?

A light microscope is a type of microscope that uses visible light and a system of lenses to magnify images of small subjects. They are also known as optical microscopes.

Types of Light Microscopes

  • Compound Light Microscope: Uses more than one lens, providing higher magnification.
  • Stereo Microscope: Provides a three-dimensional view of the sample.
  • Confocal Microscope: Uses a laser to scan layers of cells to construct a 3D image.
  • Phase-Contrast Microscope: Enhances contrast in transparent and colorless objects.
  • Fluorescence Microscope: Uses high energy light to illuminate the specimen which will then emit a lower energy light.

How to Use a Light Microscope

  1. Start with the lowest power objective lens.
  2. Place the slide on the stage and secure it.
  3. Adjust the focus until clear.
  4. Switch to higher power lenses as needed.
  5. Adjust the light intensity for clarity.
